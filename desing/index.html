Genesis Chapter 1

1In the beginning, when all things were yet unformed and the vast expanse lay silent, there arose from the deep a stirring of purpose beyond mortal reckoning. 2And the earth was without shape, an endless void wrapped in darkness, and a mighty wind swept across the face of the waters. 3Then a voice spoke into the emptiness, calling forth light from where there had been none, and light came into being, brilliant and unfailing. 4The light was seen to be good, and it was set apart from the darkness, each given its own dominion and hour. 5The light was called Day, and the darkness Night. And so evening came, and morning followed — the first day.

6Then it was spoken again: let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters, a great dome to divide the waters below from the waters above. 7And it was so, the firmament stretched out like a curtain over the deep, holding all things in their appointed place. 8The firmament was called Heaven. And evening came, and morning followed — the second day.

9And the voice spoke once more, gathering the waters beneath the heavens into one place so that dry ground might appear. 10The dry ground was called Earth, and the gathered waters were called Seas — and it was good. 11Then the earth brought forth every kind of growing thing: grasses bearing seed, trees yielding fruit, each according to its own kind. 12And the earth was clothed in green, abundant and flourishing, and it was good. 13And evening came, and morning followed — the third day.
